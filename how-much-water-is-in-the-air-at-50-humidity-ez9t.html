15. Two pairs of different sizes contain 34.5 liters of water altogether. when 0.68 liter of water is poured from the bigger pail into the smaller pail, the amount of water in the bigger pail is 9 times that in the smaller pail. how much water was there in each pail at first?


Let
a = bigger pail
b = smaller pail

So from above, we get :
a + b = 34.5...(1)
a-0.68=9(b+0.68)...(2)

By eliminating the two equations, we simply get :
b + 0.68 = 34.5 - 9b - 6.12
<=>10b = 34.5 - 6.12 - 0.68
<=>10b = 27.7
<=> b = 2.77 liters

Hence, we get a = 34.5 - 2.77 = 31.73 liters.

Thus, the answer is 31.73 liters in the bigger one and 2.77 liters in the smaller one.
