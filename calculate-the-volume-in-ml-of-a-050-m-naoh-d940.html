11. a 25 ml solution of 0.5 M NaOH is titrated until neutralized into a 50 ml sample of HCI.what was the concentration of the HCI


the concentration of HCl is 0,25 M

Discussion Section

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution by reacting with another solution which is already have known concentration (usually a standard solution). Determination of the endpoint / equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range

Titrations can be distinguished including acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. At acid base titration the principle of neutralization of acids and bases is used.

At acid base titration there will be a change in the pH of the solution. When the alkaline is titrated by acid the pH will drops, and vice versa if the acid is titrated by alkaline then the pH will rise.

From this pH change a Titration Curve can be made which is a function of acid / base volume and pH of the solution

On titration of strong acids with strong bases, the point is equivalent at pH = 7

At titration of weak acids with strong bases, the point is equivalent to a pH between 8-9

At weak base titration with strong acid, the point is equivalent to a pH between 5-6

Acid-base titration formula

Ma. Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration

Va, Vb = acid base volume

na, nb = acid base valence

Known Variable

25 ml solution of 0.5 M NaOH

50 ml sample of HCI.

Asked Variable

the concentration of the HCI

Solution/Answered

First ,Input the number already known in the Acid-base titration formula

Ma. Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb

a = NaOH, b = HCl

0,5 . 25 . 1 = Mb . 50 . 1 (acid and base valence of NaOH and HCl = 1)

Mb = 0,25 M

So the concentration of HCl is 0,25 M

14. The Kjeldahl procedure was used to analyze 256 uL of a solution containing 37.9 mg protein/mL. The liberated NH3 was collected in 5.00 mL of 0.0336 M HCl and the remaining acid required 6.34 mL of 0.010 M NaOH for complete titration. What is the weight percent of N in the protein.


Metode Kjedahl merupakan metode analisis untuk menentukan 256 mikro Liter larutan protein dengan massa jenis 37,9 mg / mL. Kandungan NH₃ dalam protein direaksikan dengan 5 mL HCl 0,0336 M dan kelebihan asam dinetralkan oleh 6,34 mL NaOH 0,01 M.

Persentase N dalam protein sebesar 15 %.

Pembahasan

Protein merupakan senyawa organik yang termasuk makromolekul. Dalam protein mengandung asam amino dan amonia (NH₃).

Berdasarkan soal diketahui :

Volume larutan = 256 mikro liter = 0,256 mL

Massa jenis larutan = 37,9 mg / mL

Massa larutan = Volume × Massa jenis

                       = 0,256 mL × 37,9 mg / mL

                       = 9,7024 mg

HCl

Volume = 5 mL

Molaritas = 0,0336 M

Mol HCl mula - mula = Volume × Molaritas

                                  = 5 mL × 0,0336 M

                                  = 0,168 mmol

NaOH

Volume = 6,34 mL

Molaritas = 0,010 M

Mol NaOH = Volume × Molaritas

                 = 6,34 mL × 0,010 M

                 = 0,0634 mmol

mol HCl yang tersisa = mol NaOH

                                   = 0,0634 mmol

mol HCl yang bereaksi = mol HCl mula - mula - mol HCl tersisa

                                      = 0,168 mmol  -  0,0634 mmol

                                      = 0,1046 mmol

Reaksi :

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

0,0634 mmol

                           NH₃ + HCl → NH₄Cl

mula - mula :         -      0,168

reaksi          :                0,1046

                   ____________________________ -

sisa              :                0,0634

mol NH₃ = mol HCl

              = 0,1046 mmol

massa NH₃ = mol × Mr

                   = 0,1046 mmol × 17

                   = 1,7782 mg

massa N dalam NH₃

= Ar N / Mr NH₃ × massa NH₃

= 14 / 17 × 1,7782 mg

= 1,4644 mg

% massa N dalam protein

= massa N / massa protein × 100%

= 1,4644 / 9,7024 × 100 %

= 15 %
