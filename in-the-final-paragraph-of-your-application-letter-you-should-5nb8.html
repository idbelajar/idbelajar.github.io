Jawaban:

First of all, don't forget to include the place and date of the letter. This may seem trivial, but by including the date, you will be considered that you are serious about looking for work. If there is no date, month and year, the company's HRD might consider your job application letter to be an old job application letter.

Next, write the destination address for your job application letter. The company name and address must be written completely and correctly.

In the opening section, state where you got the job information. Whether from newspapers, social media, websites or recommended by someone. Also state the job position you would like the company to open for job vacancies in various positions.

Write down your personal data, including your full name, place of birth, last education, address, email (if any) and telephone number.

Mention all attached files that you include in the job application letter. For example, recent photographs, photocopies of ID cards, curriculum vitae, photocopies of recent diplomas, paklaring (work experience letters), photocopies of course certificates or work training that you have attended, yellow letters, SKCK, and so on.

Mention your work experience, and the abilities you have that could support the job position you want.

At the end, show that you are enthusiastic about the job you are applying for and have a strong desire to occupy the job position.

After writing the thank you notes and greetings, don't forget to sign your job application letter.

In writing the job application letter, it is mandatory to use good and correct Indonesian according to EYD (Enhanced Spelling). Don't use slang, let alone Alay. For letters use a formal typeface, for example, Times New Roman or Arial.

Write a job application letter that is solid, clear and polite. Don't be convoluted. There is no need to be long-winded, for example explaining all educational history starting from kindergarten and early childhood education. You don't need to also write down all your hobbies and achievements, especially if it's not related to the type of job you are going to apply for.

Write your own job application letter. Do not plagiarize or completely copy job application letters circulating on the internet. You can imitate, but don't trace it whole, change it a little with your own words. Usually companies are very familiar with job application letters that are only copy and paste from the internet. And if that is the case, the HRD will immediately get rid of applications like that, because the applicant is considered lazy and not creative.

If the application letter includes an email, or if the application letter will be sent via email, avoid using the same email address. For example agungcuteabis@gmail.com or susinaqmanjah@gmail.com. Email looks very unprofessional. Use a kind and humane email address such as agungsunarko@gmail.com or susirosmala@gmail.com.

Include photos that are formal and polite, wearing a shirt or coat with a blue or red background, like the photos on the KTP. Don't use selfies with scenic backgrounds.

Do not write down your experience of having worked at a company, where you stopped working at that company because you were fired, especially if you were fired because of a fatal mistake. This makes prospective employers afraid to accept you. Worried that you might make the same mistake at his company.

Also avoid writing work experiences, but only briefly you worked at the company. This can make you feel like a boring, loyal person.

If the job application letter is handwritten, write it clearly and neatly. Don't be like a doctor's writing that is hard to read. No abbreviations. Use a ballpoint pen (pen / pen) in black or blue ink.

Finally, before being sent, reread the job application letter y
