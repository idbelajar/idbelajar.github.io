8. A rectangular courtyard 3.78 metres long and 5.25 metres wide is to be paved exactly with square tiles, all of the same size. What is the largest size of the tile which could be used for the purpose?


The largest size of tile which could be used for the purpose is 21 cm × 21 cm.

 

Explanation

For this purpose, let’s calculate in integers.

First, let’s convert the courtyard side lengths to cm.

3.78 metres long and 5.25 metres wide equals to:
378 cm long and 525 cm wide.

Second, because the tiles are all in square shape, let’s find the greatest common factor/divisor of 378 and 525.

378 = 2 × 3³ × 7525 = 3 × 5² × 7

gcf(378, 525) = 3 × 7 = 21

Therefore, the largest size of tile which could be used for the purpose is 21 cm × 21 cm.

The “formation” of the tiles should be (378/21) by (525/21) = 18 by 25 tiles.

 

CONCLUSION

∴  The largest size of tile which could be used for the purpose is 21 cm × 21 cm.
