9. what is the different between the future of shall/will and to be going to


the simple future tense is used to show future action.
we use 'shall' with I and We and 'will' with other nouns and pronouns.
shall/will: I, WE  will: YOU, HE, SHE, IT 
the 'going to' form may be used for future action, especially when a plan has been made.

e.g:
we shall play a game of chess after lunch
cece is ill. she will not be at the party
they are going to wash the car for dad

12. 1. What is a different between will and shall?2. In which sentence we use must. ought to, should, and have to ?​


Jawaban:

1. 'will' is used for affirmative and negative sentences about the future, you can also use 'will' for requests too.

If you want to make an offer or suggestion with I/we, use 'shall' in the question form. For very formal statements, especially to describe obligations, use 'shall'

2. must is used for specific obligations

example: I have to brush my teeth twice a day.

ought to is only used with reference to the present and the future, and it is rarely used in questions and negatives

example: you ought to listen carefully.

should is used to give, or ask for, advice or an opinion in the present

example: you should call him

have to mainly expresses general obligations, while must is used for specific obligations

example: In France, you have to drive on the right.
