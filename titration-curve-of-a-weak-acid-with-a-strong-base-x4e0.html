1. In an acid base titration, 17,45 ml of 0,180M nitric acid HNO3 were completely neutralized by 14,76 ml of Al(OH)3 . calculate the concentration of the Al(OH)3


[HNO3] : 0,180 M
V HNO3 : 17,45 mL
[Al(OH)3] : ........ M ?
V Al(OH)3 : 14,76 mL

Answered

Step 1
Make a reaction of each compounds

Reaction
3HNO3 + Al(OH)3 → Al(NO3)3 + 3H2O

Step 2
Determine the number of mmole of HNO3

M : mmole / mL
mmole : M x mL

mmole HNO3 : M HNO3 x V. HNO3
mmole HNO3 : 0,180 mmole/mL . 17,45 mL
mmole HNO3 : 3,141 mmole

Step 3
Determine mmole of Al(OH)3 by using the coefficient reaction of Al(OH)3 toward the coefficient reaction of HNO3 based on the reaction above.

mmol Al(OH)3 : (c. Al(OH)3 / c. HNO3) x mmole HNO3
mmole Al(OH)3 : (1/3) x 3,141 mmole
mmole Al(OH)3 : 1,047 mmole

Step 4
Determine concentration of the Al(OH)3.

M : mmole / mL

M Al(OH)3 : 1,047 mmole / 14,76 mL
: 0,071 mmole / mL
: 0,071 M

So, the concentration of Al(OH)3 is 0,071 M

2. which component of salt NaClO is the conjugate base is it weak or strong?


A conjugate base is the particle that is left over after the acid loses its hydrogen ion, thus ClO⁻ defined as conjugate base in NaClO.

How strong (or weak) conjugate base is, determined by it acid, HClO.
Due to HClO is weak acid, then ClO⁻ categorized as strong base conjugate.


Strong acid ⇄ weak base conjugate
Weak acid ⇄ strong base conjugate
