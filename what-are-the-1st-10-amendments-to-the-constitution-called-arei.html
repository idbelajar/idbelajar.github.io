The state is a first level administrative area of a federal or union state. Many countries that have a federal constitution have this region, which is sometimes called a state (state, estado), land (länder) or province (province, provincia). In most cases these state territories are formed by the federal or central government. The countries of Malaysia and the United States are exceptions in this case, because the states in Malaysia and the US were originally sovereign states themselves, but chose voluntarily to become members of a federation.

If a political entity such as a sovereign state chooses to join a federation as a state, then the state will lose their position as a political entity bound by international law.
