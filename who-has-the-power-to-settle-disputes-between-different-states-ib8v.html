Penjelasan:

SUBORDINATORS

So that and in order to are subordinators which are used to introduce a clause that expresses a purpose.  

So that + a clause follows a main clause that expresses a plan.

So that precedes a subordinating clause (S+verb+complement) that usually includes a modal (can, could, will, would, may, or might).

In order to expresses a similar meaning with so that. However, in order to is not followed by a subject+verb, but by an infinitive clause (verb+complement).

Examples:

I wake up at four so that I can see the sun rise.

I wake up at four in order to see the sun rise.  

She brings her own lunch so that she can save money.

She brings her own lunch in order to save money.

They practice hard so that they can win the match.

They practice hard in order to win the match.

14. what is the different between the future of shall/will and to be going to


the simple future tense is used to show future action.
we use 'shall' with I and We and 'will' with other nouns and pronouns.
shall/will: I, WE  will: YOU, HE, SHE, IT 
the 'going to' form may be used for future action, especially when a plan has been made.

e.g:
we shall play a game of chess after lunch
