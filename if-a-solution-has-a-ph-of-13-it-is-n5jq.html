2. 0.08 moles of sulfuric acid in 2 litres of solution has a Ph?​


n (moles) = M / V

M (concentracion) = n x V = 0,08 moles x 2 Litres = 0,16 M

Since H₂SO₄ is strong acid, we must use this formula:

[H⁺] = acid valence x Concentration

[H⁺] = 2 x 0,16 M

[H⁺]= 0,32 M = 32 x 10⁻² M

pH = - log [H⁺]

pH = - log (32 x 10⁻² M)

pH = 2 - log 32

~apply some logarithm properties~

pH = 2 - 5 log 2

pH = 0,5

That gives us pH of said acid is 2 - 5 log 2.

3. acetic acie has Ka= 10^-5 . If 0,2 moles of this acid is mixed 0,02moles of sodium acetate in one liter of water, a buffer is formed. Calculate pH of this solution .​


pH = 4

Solution :

Determining the concentration of acid

[CH3COOH] = 0,2mole/1L = 0,2M

Determining the concentration of conjugate base

[CH3COO-] = 0,02mole/1L = 0,02

Determining the concentration of H+ and pH

[H+] = Ka × [CH3COOH]/[CH3COO-]

[H+] = 10^-5 × 0,2M/0,02M = 10^-4

so :

pH = -log [H+]

pH = -log 10^-4

pH = 4

This, pH of the Solution is 4

10. A solution of H2SO4 with a molal concentration of 8.010 m has adensity of 1.354g/mL. What is the molar concentration of thissolution?​


Answer with explanation:

You are given a solution of sulphuric acid with a molal concentration of 8.010 m and its density is 1.354 g/mL.

And we also know that the molar mass of sulphuric acid which is 98.079 g/mol.

◈ ◈ ◈

With the known datas, we work out the molar concentration in mol/L.

◈ ◈ ◈

The relation between molarity, molality and density is described within the following equation.

[tex]\frac{1}{m} = \frac{d}{M}-\frac{Mr}{1000}[/tex]

Where,

Density (d) = 1.354 g/mL

Molecular mass of solute (Mr) = 98.079 g/mol

Molality (m) = 8.010 m

Molarity (M) = ... mol/L

◈ ◈ ◈

So then,

[tex]\frac{1}{8.010\ m} = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M}-\frac{98.079\ g/mol}{1000}[/tex]

[tex]0.1248\ m = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M}-0.098079\ g/mol[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M} = 0.098079\ g/mol+ 0.1248\ m[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M} = 0.222879\ g/mmol[/tex]

[tex]M = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{0.222879\ g/mmol}[/tex]

[tex]M = 6.075\ mol/L[/tex]

◈ ◈ ◈

Therefore, the molar concentration of said acid solution is 6.075 mol/L.
