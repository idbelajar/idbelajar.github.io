8. What is the different from rsvp and regret only ?


Jawaban:

RSVP is an abbreviation of the French phrase 'Repondez, s'il vous plaît'. It translates to 'Respond, if you please' or, better still, 'Respond please'. It means the sender of the letter want the reciever to reply the letter whether you can attend or not.

The phrase "RSVP, regrets only", - or simply "Regrets only", is a popular modern variation that implies "if you do not reply, that will be taken as an acceptance". So it means don't answer the letter if you want to attend, and answer the letter if you can't attend

11. What is the different from present perfect tense and simple past tense ?​


Jawaban:

kalimat present perfect tense untuk menunjukkan pengalaman dan Menunjukkan suatu kejadian yang belum selesai.

sedangkan

kalimat simple past tense menunjukkan suatu kegiatan/atau kejadian yang sudah terjadi/sudah lewat (lampau)

Penjelasan:

Present perfect tense:

Formula:

S + have/has + Past participle

kalimat:

We have worked in the hospital for two years

Simple past tense:

Formula:

S + was/were + non verb

kalimat:

She was beautiful in the party last night

terimakasih

The different: Present perfect tense is used to tell the actions that occurred at a non-specific time or used to tell the actions that started from the past and still continue to the present.Simple past tense is used to tell the action from past event.

PEMBAHASAN1. Present Perfect Tense

➡ Digunakan untuk menceritakan tindakan yang terjadi pada waktu yang tidak spesifik atau digunakan untuk menceritakan tindakan yang dimulai dari masa lalu dan masih berlanjut hingga saat ini.

➡ Rumus Present Perfect

Verbal Sentence

(+) S + has/have + V3 + O/C

(-) S + has/have + not + V3 + O/C

(?) has/have + S + V3 + O/C?

Nominal Sentence

(+) S + has/have + been + Complement

(-) S + has/have + not + been + Complement

(?) has/have + S + been + Complement?

2. Simple Past Tense

➡ Digunakan untuk menceritakan tindakan dari peristiwa masa lalu.

➡ Rumus Simple Past Tense

Verbal Sentence

(+) S + V2 + O/C

(-) S + did + not + V1 + O/C

(?) Did + S + V1 + O/C?

Nominal Sentence

(+) S + Be (was/were) + Complement

(-) S + Be (was/were) + not + Complement

(?) Be (was/were) + S + Complement?
