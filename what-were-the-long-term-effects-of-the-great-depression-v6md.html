1. What were the consequences of the Great Depression on the League of Nations? Explain more than 50 words


Jawaban:

The Great Depression as an extrinsic force only exacerbated the situation rather than having created the League’s quagmire in the first place; the League’s doom was largely resulted from intrinsic factors. The inherent weaknesses of the League prevented effective resolutions from being made, thus making countries grow mistrust and seek for solutions elsewhere. The cornerstone of the League, the Covenant, was characterized by its ambiguity and impracticality that seemed to be more problematic than the problems themselves. A notorious example would be the Article of the League’s Covenant, in which it stipulated that “national armaments should be reduced to the lowest point consistent with national safety”. Apart from this, the decision-making organ in the League, the Assembly, was highly inefficient: a unanimous vote had to be reached before any resolution could be passed, so that aggressor states sometimes completed their acts before any action could be taken. The Council’s decisions were sometimes rendered futile, as its permanent members could vote any resolution.
