Frogs are a kind of small animal belonging to a group called vertebrates (animals with backbones) known as amphibians. This mean that they live part of their life in water and the other part of it on land. The word amphibian comes from 2 Greek words, "amphi" which means "both" and "bios" which means "life". Amphibians are cold-blooded animals. This mean that their body temperature is the same as the surrounding temperature. Their skin absorbs water into their body so they do not have to drink water to survive.
Examples of other amphibians are salamanders and caecillians.
Frogs have strong hind legs to enable them leap forward at a great distance.
The front legs or arms are short. They are used to prop the frog up when it sits.

The Cricket frog (4cm or 1.5 inch long) can leap 40 times its length in one jump...

8. Passive voice is used in scientific writing to make the writings objective,and impersonal. *TrueFalseNowadays, most medical and scientific style manuals do not support theactive over the passive voice. *TrueFalseThe passive voice is not acceptable in expository writing. *TrueFalse​


1. True

2. False

3. False

ELABORATION

1. Passive voice is used in scientific writing to make the writings objective, and impersonal. (True)

➡ Based on the past history, in the 1920s, the scientists began to use the passive writing style for the particular purposes; to make their writings objective and impersonal. The scientists determine the passive writing as a standard style for medical and scientific journal publications.

2. Nowadays, most medical and scientific style manuals do not support the active over the passive voice. (False)

➡ According to the American Medical Association's AMA manual of style, the active voice is better used by the authors than the passive voice for the general purpose. It's an exception, if the author wants to focus on what is acted upon or the performer is unknown.

3. The passive voice is not acceptable in expository writing. (False)

➡ According to the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (APA), the use of passive voice is acceptable in the expository writing when the authors wanna focus on the receiver of the action or the agent is of less importance.
