5. mention and give explanation of the generic structure of Narrative text ​


Jawaban:

1. Orientation

Orientation or commonly called preliminary, contains about who, when, where a story is set.

2. Complication

Complication tells the beginning of the problem that causes the peak of the problem or commonly called the climax. This part usually involves the main character of the story.

3. Resolution

This section is the end of the story or in the form of a solution to the problem that occurred. Problems can be solved can be better or even worse which will later make the story happily end or sad ending.

4. Re-orientation

The part is the closing of an optional story. Re-orientation can contain moral lessons, suggestions or teachings from the author.

11. explain the differences of recount, spoof and narrative text


Answer: 

In English text, there are various kind of text based on its content or function. Those three are some of them. Here are the differences among them
- Recount text contains the personal experience told by the writer. Its function is to share experience, especially about a holiday, to the readers.
- Spoof text contains the parody story. Its function is to entertain the readers. 
- Narrative text contains a pure story. Its function is to tell a story which may inspire the readers.

15. write what is narrative text? and write the social function and general structure of narrative text!​


Jawaban:

Narrative text is a story with complication or problematic events and it tries to find the resolutions to solve the problems.

The social function of narrative is to amuse, entertain and to deal with an actual or vicarious experience in different ways.

There are three generic structure of narrative text, namely: Orientation, Complication, and Resolution.
