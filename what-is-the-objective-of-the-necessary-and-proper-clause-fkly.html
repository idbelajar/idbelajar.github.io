11. what is the different between sentences and clause?​


Penjelasan:

SENTENCE

A sentence is a group of words which communicate a complete thought or idea.

The most basic sentence in English contains only a subject and a verb.

I ran.

He slept.

Note: sometimes the subject may be implied: Run! (you run, implied)

—TYPES OF SENTENCE

In English, sentences start with a capital letter. They end with a full stop, question mark or exclamation mark depending on what type of sentence they are and what they are communicating – there are 4 categories:

—Statement

used when stating information (also called declarative sentences)

I have written a book.

—Question

used when asking for information (also called interrogative sentences)

Did you take his number?

—Exclamation

used when expressing emotions (also called exclamatory sentences)

I won!

—Imperative

used when giving commands (also called command sentences)

Open the door.

A sentence may contain only a couple of words, as we’ve seen above, or can be longer and have a more complex construction, and this is when clauses may be used.

CLAUSE

A clause is a group of words which contains at least a subject and a verb. In this, clauses are like sentences, and a clause may stand as a sentence if it makes sense as a complete thought.

However, clauses are not always a complete thought and so may need to be joined with other clauses to express a complete thought, and become a sentence.

—TYPES OF CLAUSE

Independent or main clause

expresses a complete thought and may stand as a sentence.

The structure is:

subject + verb

Emma arrived home.

Emma ate a sandwich.

—Dependent or subordinate clause

does not express a complete thought so can not stand on its own, and must be combined with at least an independent clause to make a complete thought and therefore a sentence.

The structure is:

subordinating conjunction + subject + verb

After Emma arrived home

note that although this contains a subject and a verb it is not a complete thought

To make the dependent clause a complete thought we need an independent clause with it:

After Emma arrived home, she ate a sandwich.

dependent clause + independent clause = complete thought

A dependent clause may come before or after the independent clause, so this sentence is just as accurate.

Emma ate a sandwich after she arrived home.

NOTE:

if the dependent clause comes before the independent clause a comma comes between the clauses:

After Emma arrived home, she ate a sandwich. (dependent clause first – comma)

Emma ate a sandwich after she arrived home. (independent clause first – no comma)

15. what is adjective clause? and give the example​


║RELATIVE CLAUSE-ADJECTIVE CLAUSE║

[tex]Answer[/tex]

Adjective clause is a dependent clause that has a function of joining two clauses in the sentence and as a modifier of a noun or pronoun.

Adjectice clause can be identified by using the relative pronoun to modify a noun, such as who, whom, which and whose.

Relative pronoun "who" describes person as a subject of the sentence.

Relative pronoun "whom" describes person as an object of the sentence.

Relative pronoun "which" describes animal or the thing whether it is as subject or an object of the sentence.

Relative pronoun "whose" describes a personal possessive or possessive pronoun.  

Relative pronoun is always attached near the described subject or noun.

Example :

1. The man whom I saw yesterday night is Mr. Kenny.

Let's break it down :

Adjective clause/dependent clause =>whom I say yesterday night

Subject/noun/person =>the man

Verb=>is

Noun =>Mr. Kenny

The girl whose black hair is my sister.

Let's break it down :

Adjective clause/dependent clause=>whose black hair

Subject/person=>the girl

Verb=>is

my sister=>noun

The library which is near my school is being constructed.

Let's break it down :

Adjective clause/dependent clause=>which is near my school

Subject/thing=>the library

verb => is being constructed

The man who wears glasses is an English teacher.

Let's break it down :

Adjective clause=>who wears glasses

Subject/noun/person=> the man

Verb=> is

Noun=>An English teacher

[tex]Explanation[/tex]

Adjective clause  merupakan anak klausa(dependent clause) yang berfungsi sebagai penggabung klausa dan penjelas  kata benda (noun) nya. Adjective clause diidentifikasi dengan penerapan bantuan relative pronoun { who;whom;which;whose}  untuk menghubungkan kedua kalimat yang memiliki informasi sama dari subyek/obyek yang dijabarkan secara detail.

Harap diingat bahwa penempatan relative pronoun selalu berada dibelakang kata benda yang dijabarkan.

≡ Who memperjelas orang sbg subyek.

Example :

" That man who wears black T-shirt is my father."

=> Relative pronoun "who" menggantikan posisi subjective pronoun "He" dan jika dijabarkan akan menjadi 2 klausa/kalimat :

That man is my father. He wears black T-shirt.

≡ Whom memperjelas orang sebagai  obyek

Example :

"The old woman whom I waited for yesterday is my neighbor."

=> Relative pronoun "whom" memperjelas obyek kata kerja nya. Relative pronoun menggantikan objective pronoun "her." Jika dijabarkan akan menjadi :

The old woman is my neighbor. I waited for her yesterday.

≡ Which memperjelas hal / benda baik sebagai subyek, obyek, obyek preposisi

Example :

"That yellow car which is parked on the backyard is mine."

=> Relative pronoun "which" menggantikan  subjective pronoun "it." Jika dijabarkan akan menjadi :

That yellow car is mine. It is parked on the backyard.

≡ Whose memperjelas kepemilikan personal

Example :

"That man whose eyes color is blue is my friend."

=> Relative pronoun "whose" menggantikan possessive pronoun "his." Jika, dijabarkan menjadi :

That man is my friend. His eyes color is blue.
