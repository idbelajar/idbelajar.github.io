1. A cubic container is filled with 1000cm³ of water. If 1197cm³ of the water is added, find the height, in cm, of water in the container.​


Short answer:

13 cm

Explanations:

Cubic container = 1.000 cm³ + 1.197 cm³

Cubic container = 2.197 cm³

Cubic container = side x side x side

2.197 cm³ = side³

side = [tex]\sqrt[3]{2.197}[/tex] cm

side = 13 cm

So, the height of water in the container is 13 cm

4. How many grams are in 1.2 x 10²⁴ molecules of water?​


Answer

Mass of water = 36 grams

Explanation

Molecules of water is written in formula : H₂O,

to calculate the mass of the water, we should know about realtive molecular mass of water from periodic table :

Mr H₂O = 2 x Ar H + Ar O = 2 x 1 + 16 = 18 g/mole

From Avogadro number ==>

[tex]\boxed{mole\ of\ water\ = \frac{Molecules\ number\ of \ Water}{6,02\ .\ 10^{23} } }[/tex]

mole of water = 1.2 x 10²⁴ /(6.02 x 10²³) = 1.99 mole = 2 mole

Mass of water = mole of water x Mr water = 2 mole x 18 g/mole

= 36 grams of water

12. Try!! 1. The density of a material is 2 kg/m³. What is its specific volume? 2. An empty 10 gal tank weighs 4.5 lb. What is the total weight of the tank plus the water when it is filled with 5 gal of water? 3. The specific gravity of a solution is 0.80 at 70°F. How many cubic feet will be occupied by 100 lb of the solution at 70°F?​


Jawaban:

1. 0.5 m³/kg, 2. 46.225 lb, 3. 125 ft³

Penjelasan:

1. Specific volume = 1/density, so 1/2 m³/kg or 0.5 m³/kg is the specific volume of the material

2. Density of water is 8.345 lb/ U.S. gal. We will assume that the gal unit is U.S. gal, so..

Total mass = tank mass + water mass = 4.5 lb + 5 gal * 8.345 lb/gal = 46.225 lb

3. Specific gravity is ratio of solution density to a reference (mainly water), so we can treat the specific gravity just like density. The volume occupied by the solution will be:

V = mass/specific gravity = 100 lb / 0.8 lb/ft³ = 125 ft³
