1. How do scientists calculate the magnitude of an earthquake (help)​


The Richter scale measures the greatest vibration (amplitude) in record, but another measure measures a different part of an earthquake. The usgs currently report the scale of earthquakes using their present size, although many other magnates are calculated for research and comparison.

English:

Seismographs have sensitive instruments that can receive seismic waves generated by earthquakes. The seismic waves that occur during the earthquake are depicted with wavy lines on the seismogram. Seismologists measure these lines and calculate the magnitude of the earthquake.

Types of Earthquakes

The types of earthquakes are divided into 2, namely based on their cause and depth. The following is an explanation:

a. Based on the Cause

According to the causes of their occurrence, earthquakes can be divided into three types, namely:

1. Volcanic Earthquake

Volcanic earthquakes are earthquakes caused by volcanic eruptions. Example: Mount Bromo earthquake, G. Una-Una earthquake, Mount Krakatau earthquake.

2. Tectonic Earthquake

Tectonic earthquakes are earthquakes that occur due to a shift in the earth's crust due to the release of energy in the subduction zone. Tectonic earthquakes are quite powerful. Example: the earthquake in Aceh, Bengkulu, Pangandaran.

3. Earthquake crushes or rocks

Earthquake collapse or earthquake is an earthquake caused by landslides, collapsed caves, and the like. This type of earthquake has only a small impact and is narrow in area.
