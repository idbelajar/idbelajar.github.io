1. Find the area of triangle​


First, find the area of the biggest triangle. With the given base and height, [tex] 18 + 8 = 26 [/tex] cm and [tex] 7 [/tex] cm, respectively, we get

[tex] \displaystyle \mathcal{A}_\text{biggest} = \frac{1}{2} \times 7 \times 26 = 91 [/tex]

and the smallest triangle, we have [tex] 8 [/tex] cm and [tex] 7 [/tex] cm, respectively, and yields

[tex] \displaystyle \mathcal{A}_{\text{smallest}} = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 7 = 28 [/tex]

If we want to find the gray area, then we should subtract the biggest with the smallest one. And it gives us

[tex] \displaystyle \underset{\text{Gray}}{\mathcal{A}} = 91 - 28 = 63 [/tex]

8. The figure is made up of an equilateral triangle of side 10 cm and a semicircle. Find the perimeter of the figure. (Take π = 3.14) 10 cm​


Jawab:

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

perimeter of 1/2 circle =1/2x2xphixr=1/2x2x3,14x10=31,4cm now perimeter of isosceles triangle=s1+s2+s3=

10+10+10=30cm=

30cm+31.4cm

= 61.4cm so the combined area of ​​a 1/2 circle with an isosceles triangle is 61.4cm

The perimeter of the figure is 35.7 cm.
 

Explanation

The perimeter of the figure is the sum of the perimeter of the semicircle and 2 times the length of the side of the triangle. The diameter of the semicircle equals to the length of the side of the triangle.

(Note that one side of the triangle lies inside the figure, so it is excluded from the sum.)

Solving it:

[tex]\begin{aligned}P&=\frac{1}{2}(\pi d)+2s\\&=\frac{1}{2}(\pi s)+2s\ \ \because d=s\\&=\left(\frac{\pi}{2}+2\right)s\\&=\left(\frac{3.14}{2}+2\right)(10\ \rm cm)\\&=\left(1.57+2\right)(10\ \rm cm)\\&=\left(3.57\right)(10\ \rm cm)\\\therefore\ P&=\boxed{\,\bf35.7\ cm\,}\ \ \sf(answer)\end{aligned}[/tex]
