The Industrial Revolution began in Britain. The British government was (1) eager to increase the country's income from trade, and it encouraged industry. Business people also hoped to make more money. They saw opportunities to (2) profit from new inventions and new ways of making goods.

British businesses began to experiment with power-driven machines. They learned that machines could make some kinds of goods more quickly, cheaply, and (3) reliably than could craft workers. Workers were still needed to operate the machines, but not to produce goods directly.

Waterpower provided the energy to run the first industrial machines. People built giant waterwheels that turned in currents of water. The wheels powered simple (4) devices, such saws and millstones for grinding wheat and corn.

Advances in metalworking allowed the invention of powerful new machines, such as steam engines. By 1850, most British industry (5) relied on coal-burning steam engines for power. In the late 1800s and early 1900s, oil-powered engines (6) replaced steam engines.

The first big factories in Britain grew up around the textile industry. For centuries, spinning yarn and (7) weaving cloth was done mostly by hand. It was slow, patient work.

Before the industrial revolution, goods were manufactured in small shops and homes. During the Industrial Revolution, manufacturing processes moved to large factories. It was brought about through the introduction of new technologies and types of transportation. As a consequence, it had important changes for the way of life of many people. The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain in the late 1700s and spread to Europe and the United States. Thanks to plenty of coal and iron, Britain was able to power and make machines for its factories. Transportation changed dramatically throughout the Industrial Revolution owing to the introduction new ways of travel including railroads, steamboats, and automobiles. As a result, the way people and products were able to travel around the country and the world changed. The Industrial Revolution caused many social changes. Because of the revolution, people moved from the country to the cities to work in factories. Fewer people worked on farms. Accordingly and became overcrowded, unsanitary, polluted, and poor workers often lived in unsafe and unhealthy conditions. Working conditions were often dangerous on account of few laws to protect workers at the time. People often had to work long hours and child labor was a common practice. By the end of the 1900s, labor unions and new laws began to create a safer working environment. In time, this led to more liberal political systems.​
